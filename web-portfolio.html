Hamline University

Hamline's design and strategy were stuck in 2004, and the pagecount had exploded to 18,000 pages. Important content was impossible to find due to the lack of navigation oversite.

Our team strategized and implemented a complete overhaul: usability, design, structure, content, strategy. Design firm Azul 7 created the overall design and a handful of templates. I led the front-end, user experience, information architecture, branding, design, and content strategy elements.

  • Redesigned homepage and persistent navigation drive 243% more traffic to revenue-generating pages.
  • SEO best practices were implemented, drastically improving internal and external search results.
  • Final site is friendly, inviting, and engaging, earning a B+ from EDUcheckup with scores of 93 and 94 for design and content.

School of Business MBA

The old MBA site did not have its own left navigation and key links were minimized. Site search data in Google Analytics confirmed that visitors struggled to find key information.

I designed and implemented a major redesign of the MBA site's navigation and content. Based on research, we knew prospective applicants prioritized tuition, outcomes, and day-to-day program details.

  • Conceptualized and strategized an MBA-specific left menu, with sections for degree info, tuition and financial aid, and admission.
  • Swapped the old text-heavy template with a rotating banner template, creating more high-profile opportunities to market specific elements of the program.
  • Highlighted content on the homepage that appeared frequently in internal search data: tuition, assistantships, program schedule.
  • Incorporated graduate testimonials and embedded videos of former students and faculty talking about the benefits of the program.
