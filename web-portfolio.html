Hamline University

The Problem: Hamline needed a complete overhaul to remain competitive in a crowded market during the recession. Easier said than done, with a content management system that died in 2005, a design stuck in 2006, and no centralized usability and best practices oversite—since ever.

The Solution: I led the front-end development, user experience, information architecture, branding, design, content strategy, and training during an 11-month push. The entire site was rebuilt from the ground up. The redesigned homepage and persistent navigation drive 243% more traffic to revenue-generating pages. SEO best practices were implemented, drastically improving internal and external search results. Final site is friendly, inviting, and engaging, earning a B+ from EDUcheckup with scores of 93 and 94 for design and content.

Toolkit: Ektron CMS400.net CMS. Template design by Azul 7. JQuery-powered carousels. MS Visio for wireframes and sitemaps.

School of Business MBA

The Problem: The MBA program was struggling to fill seats. Industry research concluded that a graduate program's website is the #1 tool that prospective students use when making a decision. Hamline's website lacked in key areas: it did not have its own left navigation, and key information such as tuition rates and program schedules weren't discoverable.

The Solution: I put in place an MBA-specific left menu to make it as easy as possible for prospective students to find key information. I built new pages for program info, tuition and financial aid options, and how to apply instructions. The old, text-heavy homepage template was replaced with a rotating banner template, creating high-profile opportunities to push marketing messages. The new template also highlighted content that appeared frequently in internal search data.

Toolkit: Ektron CMS400.net CMS. JQuery-powered carousel. HTML, CSS for landing pages. Google Analytics for user behaviour info.

Undergraduate Admission

The Problem: I was tasked with ongoing maintenance of the Undergraduate Admission site, the most revenue-generating area of the university.

The Solution: Carousel banner constantly updated with latest news, upcoming events. Strong focus site-wide on tuition and financial aid information. Easy access to application and inquiry forms (hugely important for lead generation) through buttons and the persistent left menu. In March 2012, I led the integration of Hobsons Connect, a CRM (customer relationship management) system. I strategized, designed, and developed event listing and registration, new dynamic inquiry forms, and a customized VIP page (student portal) based on inquiry form submission. Hamline's site was ranked in top 3% of admission sites nationwide for the 2012-2013 year. [cite via Pam]
