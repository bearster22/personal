Hamline University

The Problem: Hamline needed a complete overhaul to remain competitive in a crowded market during the recession. Easier said than done, with a content management system that died in 2005, a design stuck in 2006, and no centralized usability or best practices oversite.

The Solution: I led the front-end development, user experience, information architecture, branding, design, content strategy, and training during an 11-month CMS migration and ground-up rebuild. Final site is friendly, inviting, and engaging, earning a B+ overall score from EDUcheckup.

School of Business MBA

The Problem: The MBA program was struggling to fill seats. Industry research concluded that a graduate program's website is the #1 tool that prospective students use when making a decision. Hamline's website lacked in key areas: it did not have its own left navigation, and key information such as tuition rates and program schedules weren't discoverable.

The Solution: I put in place an MBA-specific left menu to make it as easy as possible for prospective students to find key information. I built new pages for program info, tuition and financial aid options, and how to apply instructions. The old, text-heavy homepage template was replaced with a rotating banner template, creating high-profile opportunities to push marketing messages. The new template also highlighted content that appeared frequently in internal search data.

Toolkit: Ektron CMS400.net CMS. JQuery-powered carousel. HTML, CSS. Google Analytics.
